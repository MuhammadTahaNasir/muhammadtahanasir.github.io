The Deployment Rollercoaster

Building AI models? That’s the easy part, like acing a math quiz. Getting them online? Bro, that’s like juggling flaming torches while riding a unicycle blindfolded. During the Headstarter AI SWE Fellowship in July 2024, I dove into the wild world of deployment, fueled by chai and late-night Zoom calls from Pakistan to New York. I wrestled with Flask, Streamlit, and Vercel to get my AI projects live, including a pretty dope AI Roadmap Generator. Here’s my newbie take on the deployment game, with a few laughs and plenty of facepalms.

Flask: The Overachiever’s Framework

Flask is like that friend who insists on baking a cake from scratch when you just want cupcakes. It’s a Python framework that’s super flexible but expects you to be a web dev guru. I used Flask to deploy a text classifier during the fellowship. Installing it was chill with pip install flask, but then came the HTML templates and routes, and I was like, “Yo, I just want my model to say hi on the internet!”

Here’s a quick Flask app to serve an AI model:


from flask import Flask, request, render_template
app = Flask(__name__)

@app.route('/', methods=['GET', 'POST'])
def predict():
    if request.method == 'POST':
        input_text = request.form['text']
        # Your AI model would strut its stuff here
        prediction = "Positive vibe detected!"
        return render_template('index.html', prediction=prediction)
    return render_template('index.html')

if __name__ == '__main__':
    app.run(debug=True)

Deploying on Heroku was like teaching a cat to fetch possible, but painful. The Procfile was fussier than my auntie at a wedding, and my model’s dependencies made the app slower than a Karachi traffic jam. After some Stack Overflow marathons, I trimmed the fat, but Flask taught me: you get control, but you gotta earn it.

Streamlit: My Python Pal

Streamlit is the homie who says, “You know Python? You’re basically a web dev!” It’s made for folks like me who’d rather debug a neural net than a CSS file. I used it for our AI Roadmap Generator, and bro, it was like finding biryani after a long day. Install with pip install streamlit, write a few lines, and bam, you’ve got a web app.

Here’s a simple Streamlit app for an AI model:


import streamlit as st

st.title("AI Magic Box")
user_input = st.text_input("Drop some text, fam:")
if st.button("Hit Me With a Prediction"):
    # Your AI model would flex here
    st.write("Prediction: You’re gonna love Streamlit!")

Deploying on Streamlit’s platform was smoother than my best cricket swing, thanks to my teammate Awwab, the deployment king. We hit a Firebase auth bug sessions vanishing like my Wi-Fi during load shedding but a quick SDK tweak fixed it. Streamlit’s perfect for quick AI apps, but don’t expect it to let you redesign the whole UI.

Vercel: The Cool Kid’s Deployment

Vercel is the TikTok star of deployment means flashy, user-friendly, and obsessed with Next.js. I used it for my fellowship portfolio and an AI Flashcards app. With npm install -g vercel and a Git push, your app’s live faster than you can say “viral.” It’s a dream for frontend-heavy projects but gets a bit cranky with beefy Python AI models.

Here’s a sample vercel.json for a Next.js AI app:


{
  "version": 2,
  "builds": [
    {
      "src": "next.config.js",
      "use": "@vercel/next"
    }
  ],
  "routes": [
    {
      "src": "/(.*)",
      "dest": "/"
    }
  ]
}

Vercel’s auto-scaling is slicker than my cousin’s new haircut, but I hit a snag with serverless function timeouts for my AI backend. Vercel was like, “Bro, I’m for shiny UIs, not your chunky models.” Still, for non-Python-heavy apps, it’s a total flex.

My Verdict: Who Wins?

Flask is the control freak who demands you code every pixel, it's great if you’re a web dev ninja, but I’m still a padawan. Streamlit is my ride-or-die for Python-based AI apps; it’s fast, simple, and got our AI Roadmap Generator live without me pulling my hair out. Vercel’s the cool kid, perfect for flashy frontends but not your go-to for Python-heavy AI. As a beginner, I’m vibing with Streamlit, it’s like coding with training wheels. Flask and Vercel? I’ll hit you up when I level up.

Where I’m Headed

Deployment’s like trying to land a spaceship in a storm seems tricky, but I’m hooked. I’m diving deeper into AI and web dev, chasing more projects that make people go, “Yo, that’s dope!”. Stick around for more coding chaos. Taddaaa!
