AWS Cloud Club Beginnings

Assalam o Alaikum, fam! April 2024 was a whirlwind. Started with our uni’s AWS Cloud Club launch, and man, it was like stepping into a new world. They were talking about cloud stuff like EC2, S3, Lambda and I was just trying to keep my head above water. Scribbled notes like I was back in school, but the vibe was so cool, everyone hyped about building projects. I found some free AWS tutorials online at aws site, and they were a lifesaver for understanding the basics. Got me thinking, “Okay, time to make something!”

My First Hackathon at GIKI

On 19 April, I went for my first hackathon at GIKI, called Micathon. Was I nervous? Oh, yeah. But I thought, “Let’s just do this.” It was a mess of ideas my team was throwing out plans, coding like crazy, and trying not to break everything. We used some AWS stuff I learned from the club. Our project didn’t win (it was a bit of a disaster, lol), but I learned so much about AI-related ideas. Already looking forward to the next one. Pro tip: AWS’s free training on their site helped me prep for this.

FPSC Mock and Coding Prep

Then, on 22 April, our uni had a mock for FPSC, a competitive programming contest. I’m not some coding wizard, but I gave it a shot. DOM Judge was the platform, and it was intense solving problems with a clock ticking felt like a race. My code kept failing, but I picked up some algorithm tricks. Codeforces has some great practice problems if you’re into this stuff. Got advice from seniors to stay calm next time, and I’m ready to improve.

Semester Wrap and Summer Plans

Second semester’s almost over, and finals are coming in May. After that, it’s summer break, and I’m pumped. Planning to keep coding, maybe fix that hackathon project, and write more about my tech journey. I’ve got some goals in mind, and I’ll share them in my next posts. April was wild, but it’s got me ready for more. For now, I’m just grinding, learning, and keeping it real.

“Code crash? Fix it. Brain crash? Learn from it.”
