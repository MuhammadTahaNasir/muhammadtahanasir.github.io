The Session That Got Me Thinking

سلام، ٹیک فیم! So, I moved to the hostel and thought, time to level up! That’s when I caught a 1.5 hour session on stage, not some boring lecture, just smart folks chatting about AI ethics. It hit me like a chai spill on my notebook! Got curious, so I dug into articles from places like MIT Technology Review and The Guardian, plus some random PDFs I found online. I scribbled notes, mixed them with my own thoughts, and now I’m here, your friendly tech blogger, ready to share the scoop on why AI ethics isn’t just talk!

Unpacking AI Bias

AI can be a bit of a drama queen, you know? Those articles showed me how it messes up. Picture facial recognition tech flopping on darker skin tones because the data was mostly light faces. Or a job algorithm tossing out women’s resumes since it learned from male heavy datasets. As a coder playing with NLP and ML, like I mentioned in my last post, I realized my projects could accidentally carry these traps. One article talked about a chatbot picking up toxic slang from the internet, yikes! It’s like AI mirrors our worst habits unless we clean it up. Check these out for more:

  • MIT Technology Review: How biased data skews AI results.
    AI Bias Black Box
  • The Guardian: Amazon’s scrapped AI tool and lessons learned.
    Amazon AI Bias

Ethics in the Real World

Ethics isn’t just a fancy word, it’s the soul of AI. Those articles and the session made it clear: AI shapes lives. Imagine an AI misdiagnosing someone in a hospital because of bad data, or a loan system unfairly rejecting people. The session folks said it’s like cooking biryani, get the ingredients wrong, and the whole dish flops! As a uni coder struggling with C and Python under crazy pressure, I’m starting to see how I can build AI that’s fair. It’s tough, but coding something that helps instead of hurts? That’s my goal. The articles suggested audits and diverse data, stuff I’ll try when I’m better at this.

My Journey So Far

Big win, fam! Sessional 2 exams went great, and my results were solid. But programming? Still a struggle. The uni grind and late night debugging are no joke, and I know I need to level up my skills. This AI ethics stuff feels like a perfect side project to grow with. I’m reading, experimenting, and dreaming of an ethical ML project to share soon. Stay tuned for more!
