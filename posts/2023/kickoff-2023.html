A New Year, New Chaos

Yo, it’s February 2023, and I’m kicking off my blog for the year! Last year was wild, messing with Python, Java, and getting humbled by LeetCode. Now, I’m staring down a big summer: university entrance tests. The goal’s a solid CS program at a good uni, but real talk, I’m juggling college assignments, self-learning, and test prep like a circus clown. Here’s how I’m holding it together.

AI and ChatGPT’s Spell

AI’s got me hooked, and it’s not just me chasing hype. Last year, I was all about self-driving cars and chatbots, but when ChatGPT showed up in November, it was a game-changer. I’d ask it to fix my Python code or explain a tricky LeetCode problem, and it’d break it down like a friend who’s got all the time in the world. It even helped me brainstorm a college project on recommendation systems. AI went from cool idea to something I could actually work with.

This year, I’m playing with Scikit-learn to build simple ML models. ChatGPT’s knack for explaining tough stuff makes AI feel like a puzzle I can solve, one script at a time.

Python: My Ride-or-Die

Python’s my go-to, hands down. It’s clean, quick, and perfect for AI stuff. Libraries like NumPy, Pandas, and Scikit-learn let me mess with data and ML without losing my mind over syntax like I did with Java. Last month, I made a small script to predict my study hours for test prep using Pandas. Felt like I was hacking my own life.

Here’s a bit of that script, just for kicks:

import pandas as pd
from sklearn.linear_model import LinearRegression

# Predicting study hours based on past data
data = {'Day': [1, 2, 3, 4], 'Hours': [2, 3, 2.5, 4]}
df = pd.DataFrame(data)
X = df[['Day']]
y = df['Hours']
model = LinearRegression().fit(X, y)
print(f"Predicted hours for Day 5: {model.predict([[5]])[0]:.2f}")

Python’s my way into AI. It’s where I test ideas, build models, and keep learning without wanting to throw my laptop out the window.

Balancing Uni Prep, College, and Coding

Real talk: juggling college, self-learning, and uni test prep is like playing Tetris on nightmare mode. College exams eat my time, LeetCode still makes me question my life choices, and entrance test books are thicker than my patience. But I’m managing. Mornings are for test prep, mostly math and logic problems. Afternoons go to college assignments, and nights are for coding or AI stuff.

Self-learning keeps me going. FreeCodeCamp and Tech With Tim help me stay sharp with Python, while ChatGPT’s there to fix my code or explain concepts. Consistency’s tough, but I’m aiming for one LeetCode problem a day. Slow progress is still progress, right?

What’s Next for 2023

2023’s gonna be big. By summer, I’ll wrap up my coding classes and finish my course, which is a huge milestone. I’m aiming for a uni that’ll let me go deeper into CS and AI. I want to play around with NLP, maybe build a small chatbot of my own. Once I’m in uni, I’ll be dealing with lectures and side projects, so I’ll share how I manage that chaos. I’m also starting to think about the bigger stuff, like how to make sure AI is used right. I’ll keep blogging about my projects, my mess-ups, and all the questions AI’s got me asking. My goal? Stay steady, keep learning, and maybe get some sleep.

To anyone else chasing their tech dreams: you don’t need to have it all sorted. One line of code, one problem solved, that’s enough to keep going. Keep at it, bro.
