Introduction

I’m writing this from Makkah during Umrah in this blessed month of Ramadan, March 2025. Being in one of the holiest places brings peace, but my heart’s heavy with some sad news. All my online accounts which includes Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, GitHub were hacked a few days back. Years of hard work, especially my LinkedIn with all my posts and connections, are gone. Despite this, I’m surrounded by the serenity of this sacred place, and I’m holding onto hope and faith. بہت دکھ ہوا، لیکن یہ بھی سچ ہے کہ ہر چیز میں اللہ کی مرضی ہے۔

The Hacking Incident

The hacker changed my LinkedIn profile name to “Amy Zhang” and replaced my info with fake details, erasing my entire journey. It’s tough to see years of effort vanish. I don’t know if I’ll recover my accounts, but I’m trusting Allah and pushing forward.

My AI and ML Journey

So, for those who are reding here first time, I wanna let you know that I’m a sophomore who's into Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning from the last 2-3 yeras, aiming to become an AI Engineer. This semester, I explored databases writing queries in MySQL and SQLite, modeling relationships in Neo4j, and learning how vector databases power modern apps (which surely make a detailed post on it the next coming post). It’s been a journey of understanding how data is stored, connected, and used in real-world systems. These skills are steps toward my dream, and setbacks like this won’t stop me.

  • Learned SQL for efficient data querying.
  • Explored Neo4j for graph-based relationships.
  • Studied vector databases for AI-driven applications.

Lessons from the Setback

This hacking mess taught me some hard lessons. Here’s what I’m taking away:

  • Secure Everything: Use strong passwords and two-factor authentication everywhere. I learned this the hard way.
  • Backup Your Work: I’m saving my projects and posts offline now, so I don’t lose everything again.
  • Keep Faith: Being in Makkah reminds me to trust Allah’s plan, even when things go wrong.
  • Stay Resilient: I belive that “جو گر کر دوبارہ اٹھتے ہیں، وہی اصل میں جیتتے ہیں۔” Falling down sucks, but getting up is what counts.

These lessons are keeping me grounded, even when I feel like giving up.

What’s Next

Inshallah, I’m not letting this stop me. I'd be chasing my dream of becoming an AI Engineer. I’m working on recovering my accounts, but even if I don’t, I’ll be rebuilding my online presence from scratch. This Ramadan, I’m praying for strength and guidance in Makkah. The hacking incident is a setback, but it’s not the end. Thanks for being part of my journey. I’ll keep writing and sharing as I move forward, more determined than ever. Stay tuned! Inshallah, I’ll be back with more updates. Dua mein yaad rakhna! :)
