Breaking into DSA with LeetCode: The Real Struggle Behind the Grind

September 20, 2022 • 3 min • Muhammad Taha Nasir
Table of Contents
  • The Starting Point
  • The Reality of Struggling
  • Python Is My Weapon
  • What Makes This So Hard
  • The Road Ahead

The Starting Point

So, I'm back after too many months and starting today my Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) on LeetCode. After choosing CS over AI and getting comfy with Python thanks to Zia Sir, I thought LeetCode would be the next step. Real problems, real logic, real improvement. But man, this rabbit hole goes deep.

The Reality of Struggling

You don’t just learn DSA you fight with it. Some days, I’d spend hours on one problem, only to realize I messed up the question. Arrays throw me off keeping track of indexes is a headache. Loops, especially nested ones, turn into mental mazes. Debugging’s my new routine. But every “wrong answer” teaches me something. Struggling isn’t weakness but it’s proof I’m learning for real.

Python Is My Weapon

Python’s my go-to language from those institute classes with Zia Sir quick to write, easy to read, and it lets me test ideas fast. But DSA doesn’t care about pretty code. It’s all about logic and efficiency. On LeetCode, even simple stuff like summing an array needs smart thinking, not just syntax. Zia Sir showed me Python’s power, but now I see it’s about building a sharper mind too.

What Makes This So Hard

Let’s be real tech isn’t my whole life. Mock exams at college are killing my focus after all I'm an engineering student. I’ve tried sitting with LeetCode, but papers and deadlines pull me away. I’m being inconsistent, and it’s tough to admit, but I can’t keep up right now. Maybe it’s the exams,maybe I haven’t practiced enough either way, my head’s not in it with all this going on.

The Road Ahead

I’m not at some “100 LeetCode Streak” yet, and that’s okay. I’ve started, and I’m not quitting. After exams, I’ll try again maybe one problem a day, breaking DSA into small bits, and asking Zia Sir for tips. This is my third post after talking about CS and Python, and it’s a marker of where I’m at. The real win isn’t solving fast but it’s not giving up when it feels impossible.

To anyone else grinding their tech journey: your struggles are real, your progress counts. Keep going, bro.

Real growth isn’t loud. Sometimes, it looks like one failed submission after another. And still choosing to try again the next day.
